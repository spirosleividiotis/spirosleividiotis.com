Context

Flows guide users through a logical series of steps to create or update objects. Consistent motion patterns across these steps help users understand progression, maintain context, and reduce confusion.

Patterns & Rationale

Start Flow

Transition: Slide in from bottomDirection: Bottom → CenterRationale: Entering a new flow should feel focused and intentional. Sliding in from the bottom elevates the user into a dedicated space for the task, matching platform conventions for modals and sheets.

Patterns & Rationale

Start Flow

Transition: Slide in from bottom
Direction: Bottom → Center
Rationale: Entering a new flow should feel focused and intentional. Sliding in from the bottom elevates the user into a dedicated space for the task, matching platform conventions for modals and sheets.

Next Step

Transition: Slide in from rightDirection: Right → CenterRationale: Progressing to the next step in a flow should feel like moving forward in a sequence. Right-to-left motion reinforces the mental model of moving deeper into a process, aligning with standard navigation patterns.

Previous Step

Transition: Slide in from leftDirection: Left → CenterRationale: Returning to a previous step is mapped to left-to-right motion, reinforcing the concept of retracing steps or going back. This is familiar from both iOS navigation and most web flows.

End Flow / Dismiss

Transition: Slide out to bottom or fadeDirection: Center → BottomRationale: Completing or dismissing a flow uses a downward slide to indicate returning to the main context. Fading is used for success screens to soften the transition and reinforce a sense of completion.

Enter Sub-flow

Transition: Slide in from rightDirection: Right → CenterRationale: Entering a sub-flow (e.g., adding an object within a main flow) follows the same forward motion as a next step, maintaining progression and context.

Exit Sub-flow

Transition: Slide out to leftDirection: Center → LeftRationale: Exiting a sub-flow reverses the previous movement, guiding the user back to the main flow and reinforcing spatial memory.

Success Page

Transition: Fade in/outRationale: Success screens use fading transitions to indicate a soft, positive completion without suggesting spatial movement.

Platform Notes

  • On mobile, most flow transitions follow OS conventions (e.g., modal presentations, navigation controllers).
  • On web, transitions are implemented to match the same logic, ensuring consistency across platforms.
