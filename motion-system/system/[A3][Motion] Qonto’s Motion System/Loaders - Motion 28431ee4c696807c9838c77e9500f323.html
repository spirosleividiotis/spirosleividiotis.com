Loaders - Motion

What is a loading state?

A loader is a visual indicator shown when the app or a component is fetching, processing, or rendering data. It reassures users that the system is working and prevents them from thinking the app is frozen or broken.

Why fade out is critical

  • Premium feel:¬†Smooth transitions align with Portfolio's brand positioning
  • Reduces jarring experience:¬†Abrupt cuts feel unpolished, especially for fintech
  • Globally applicable:¬†Fade-out can be applied to all loaders without complex routing logic
  • Perceived performance:¬†Smooth transitions make loading feel faster and more intentional

Why fade in Is recommended but optional

  • Technical complexity:¬†Fade-in on new content may require routing/navigation changes
  • Diminishing returns:¬†Fade-out alone solves 80% of the UX issue
  • Platform differences:¬†Easier to implement globally on web than mobile

Why skip loaders for fast loads

  • Avoid flicker:¬†Showing a loader for <400ms creates visual noise
  • Feels faster:¬†Instant content appearance is better than brief loader flash
