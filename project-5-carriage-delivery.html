Carriage, a Delivery Hero brand, needed motion design for their entire delivery experience—from the moment an order is placed to when it arrives at the customer's door. I created a cohesive animation system that guided users through every stage, providing clarity, reassurance, and delight throughout the journey.

2022 Delivery Hero Concept Work
Context

The Brief

Delivery apps live and die by user confidence. Customers need to know: Is my order confirmed? Is it being prepared? Where is it now? Motion design became the language to communicate these states—not through text alone, but through purposeful, expressive animation.

The challenge: design a motion system that worked across all delivery phases, maintained brand personality, and performed well on diverse devices.

Delivery Journey

Animation System Across Phases

I broke the delivery experience into key phases, each with its own animation personality and user need. The animations weren't decorative—they were functional communication.

[Out for Delivery]

[Nearby]
Nearby

[Issue/Delay]
Issue/Delay

Design Approach

Cohesive Yet Distinct

Each phase had its own motion personality, but they all shared a common language: smooth easings, consistent timing principles, and a focus on clarity over decoration.

The animations were built as Lottie files—lightweight, performant, and implementable across platforms. Every animation was designed with technical constraints in mind: file size limits, battery impact, and animation smoothness on lower-end devices.

The result was a delivery experience that felt responsive, trustworthy, and human—motion that guided users through uncertainty and turned waiting into engagement.

Impact

Motion as Communication

This project demonstrated how motion design can carry UX weight. The animations didn't just make the app prettier—they reduced anxiety, clarified status, and built trust during an experience where users have limited control.

By designing for the entire delivery journey, the motion system became a cohesive storytelling tool, turning a transactional process into an experience users could follow, understand, and trust.
