Case 01

Multi-Step Progressive Bar

Progress indicator for multi-step flows. Steps overlap by 250ms for seamless progression. Custom cubic-beziers at three keyframes: quick start, smooth mid-transition, confident finish.

Interactive Preview
UX Challenge
Why overlap steps? Users perceive discrete jumps as loading breaks. Starting step 2 before step 1 finishes makes progression feel continuous—the system is always working, never waiting.

Technical Specs

Property Specification
Duration motion-duration-1500 1500ms
Delay motion-delay-0 for Step 1
Step 2: 1250ms (starts 250ms before Step 1 ends)
Step 3: 2500ms (same overlap pattern)
Easing Custom cubic-bezier keyframes:
At start [0ms]: 0.67, -0.02, 0.8, 0.15
At mid [600ms]: 0.03, 0.07, 0, 1
At end [1500ms]: 0, 0.8, 0.2, 1
Scale Custom scale keyframes:
At start [0ms]: 0%
At mid [600ms]: 75%
At end [1500ms]: 100%
Multi-Step Rule
Each step starts 250ms before the previous ends. Step 1 ends at 1500ms → Step 2 delay = 1250ms.
Case 02

Tertiary Button Arrow

Text link with animated arrow. On hover: arrow slides out and back in within a mask. On click: scales down/up for tactile feedback.

Interactive Preview
UX Challenge
Why dual arrows? Two arrows in sequence create the illusion of continuous motion. 150ms stagger prevents overlap. Scale down/up provides haptic-like feedback without physical press.

Technical Specs

Interaction Specification
Click Scale Duration: motion-duration-300 300ms
Delay: motion-delay-0
Easing: motion-easing-smooth 0.4, 0, 0.6, 1
Scale: 100% → 90% (15 frames) → 100% (30 frames)
Hover In
(First Arrow)		 Duration: motion-duration-300 300ms
Delay: motion-delay-0
Easing: motion-easing-smooth 0.4, 0, 0.6, 1
Translate X: 0px → 18px
Translate Y: 0px → -18px
Hover In
(Second Arrow)		 Duration: motion-duration-300 300ms
Delay: motion-delay-150 150ms
Easing: motion-easing-smooth 0.4, 0, 0.6, 1
Translate X: 18px → 0px
Translate Y: -18px → 0px
