Case 01

Multi-Step Progressive Bar

Progress indicator for multi-step flows. Steps overlap by 250ms for seamless progression. Custom cubic-beziers at three keyframes: quick start, smooth mid-transition, confident finish.

Interactive Preview
UX Challenge
Why overlap steps? Users perceive discrete jumps as loading breaks. Starting step 2 before step 1 finishes makes progression feel continuous—the system is always working, never waiting.

Multi-Step Rule
Each step starts 250ms before the previous ends. Step 1 ends at 1500ms → Step 2 delay = 1250ms.
Case 02

Tertiary Button Arrow

Text link with animated arrow. On hover: arrow slides out and back in within a mask. On click: scales down/up for tactile feedback.

Interactive Preview
UX Challenge
Why dual arrows? Two arrows in sequence create the illusion of continuous motion. 150ms stagger prevents overlap. Scale down/up provides haptic-like feedback without physical press.

Case 03

Real-time Usage Limits Upsell

Upsell card that appears after 4 seconds on transactions screen. Card slides up with fade-in while transactions table pushes down—creating spatial awareness that content is being inserted, not overlaid.

Animation Preview
UX Challenge
Why push the table down? Z-index alone would overlay the card, but users would miss the spatial relationship. By translating the table down simultaneously, we show that the card is part of the content, not floating above it.

Case 04

Animated Text Swipe Reveal

Continuous text reveal behind a mask for personalized account setup. Each text sequence displays for 2.5s minimum. Creates loading progression while maintaining readability.

Interactive Preview
UX Challenge
Why mask the text? A simple cross-fade would be faster, but masking creates the sense of content "loading" from below—reinforcing the system is working. The 300ms position + 150ms fade-in (delayed 150ms) creates smooth sequential reveals.
