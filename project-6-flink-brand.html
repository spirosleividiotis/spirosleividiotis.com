Flink, a fast-growing grocery delivery startup, needed motion design for their core brand touchpoints—logo animation, app endscreen, and splashscreen. The brief: create animations that communicated speed, reliability, and energy while maintaining technical feasibility for fast-paced product development.

2022 Flink Brand Animation Guidelines
Context

The Challenge

Flink's brand promise was fast delivery—groceries in minutes. The motion design had to reflect that: snappy, energetic, immediate. But it also had to work at scale: lightweight files, performant animations, and designs that wouldn't slow down app load times.

I focused on three key deliverables: a logo animation for brand moments, an app endscreen for post-order confirmation, and a splashscreen for app launch—each serving a specific user need and brand moment.

Deliverables

Core Brand Animations

Design Approach

Fast, Light, Energetic

Every animation was designed with speed as a constraint. I kept timings tight—most animations under 1 second. File sizes were optimized (Lottie exports under 30KB). Colors and shapes were simplified for performance.

The motion language was consistent: sharp, confident movements with quick easings. Nothing felt sluggish or over-animated. The brand moved like it delivered—fast, precise, and reliable.

These weren't just brand decorations—they were functional touchpoints that reinforced Flink's value proposition at key moments in the user journey.

Impact

Motion as Brand Identity

These animations became part of Flink's brand toolkit—used across the app, marketing materials, and internal presentations. They gave the brand a consistent motion personality: energetic, fast, and confident.

More importantly, they demonstrated how motion design can work within tight constraints—startup timelines, performance budgets, and rapid iteration cycles—while still delivering polished, on-brand results.
